<h1 align='center'>Data Entry Introduction</h1>

<h3>1. What is Data Entry?</h3>

**Data entry** is the process of inputting, updating, or maaging information in computer systems or databases. This can include:

- Typing information from forms, invoices, or surveys into software.
- Maintaining records in spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets).
- Updating customer info, inventories, or financial data.
- Sometimes scanning documents or transcribing audio to text.

It's often entry-level but requires **accuracy, speed, and attention to detail**. Keeping information organized and accessible.

---

<h3>2.Skills you Need</h3>

**A. Technical Skills**

1. **Typing speed & accuracy** - Aim for at least 40-60 words per minute. Tools: [TypingClub](https://www.typingclub.com/) , [10FastFingers](https://10fastfingers.com/typing-test/english) .
2. **Spreadsheet software** - Excel or Google Sheets: know basic formulas, copy/paste, sorting, filtering. Key Skills: <code>SUM, AVERAGE, IF</code>, sorting, filtering, formatting cells.
3. **Word processing** – Microsoft Word or Google Docs for simple text entry.
4. **Data management software** – Some jobs require knowledge of CRMs (Customer Relationship Management) like Salesforce or Zoho, but basic jobs usually focus on Excel/Sheets.
5. **Attention to detail** – You need to avoid errors; double-check your work.
6. **Basic computer skills** – Using folders, saving files, copy-paste, and simple file organization.

**B. Soft Skills**

- Focus & patience – You’ll be entering lots of repetitive data.

- Time management – Some jobs are timed or have quotas.

- Communication – Sometimes you need to report mistakes or clarify info.
